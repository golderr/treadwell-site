# The Comp Conversation with Jesse

This is the most important conversation in the launch. Get this right and the structure protects both of you. Get it wrong and it creates resentment, ambiguity, and legal exposure.

## The setup

Have this conversation in person. Print the visual phase explainer (separate document) and walk through it together. Don't do it over text or phone.

## What to cover

### Phase 1 -- Pre-formation co-founder period

- Both of you are putting in unpaid effort to get Treadwell off the ground.
- This phase ENDS at the first paid Treadwell job.
- No wages owed during this period because there's no operating business yet.
- Sign a short pre-formation founder's letter to document this.

### Phase 2 -- W-2 production-based pay (no base salary)

- Starts at first paid job.
- Jesse becomes a W-2 employee of Treadwell Restoration LLC.
- Pay structure: a defined percentage of each job's revenue.
  - **Mitigation jobs:** 40% of gross revenue
  - **Reconstruction jobs:** 30% of gross revenue
- No base salary during this phase.
- Treadwell handles payroll taxes, workers' comp.
- Minimum wage true-up via payroll software (Gusto) -- protects against any wage and hour issue.
- Jesse can keep his personal B license active and take side jobs not through Treadwell.

### Phase 3 -- Base salary + reduced production share

- Triggers when monthly Treadwell revenue exceeds a defined threshold (e.g., $30K) for 3 consecutive months.
- Add a base salary of $5K/month.
- Production share drops to 30% mitigation / 25% reconstruction.
- Net result: more stable income for Jesse, manageable fixed cost for Treadwell.

## What Jesse gets out of this structure

- No financial risk during pre-revenue period (he's not paying out of pocket).
- Per-job earnings comparable to a 50/50 subcontractor split.
- W-2 protections: workers' comp, unemployment eligibility, employer-paid payroll taxes.
- Path to base salary stability as the business grows.
- His personal license stays his -- he can pursue other work outside Treadwell.

## What Nate gets out of this structure

- Retains 100% equity ownership.
- Compensation costs scale with revenue (no fixed payroll burden in early months).
- Legitimate W-2 employment relationship that survives CSLB and labor commissioner scrutiny.
- A qualifying individual on the LLC contractor's license.

## What both of you should agree to in writing

- Pre-formation founder's letter (Phase 1)
- Employment Agreement (Phase 2 and 3 mechanics)
- Operating Agreement (LLC structure, Jesse as Manager not Member)
- Non-solicitation provision
- Clear path forward if either party wants to exit

## Common questions Jesse might ask

**"What if the business never gets to Phase 3?"**
Then Phase 2 continues. He's never trapped at no-base-salary forever -- he's compensated per job, just no guaranteed monthly income. If that doesn't work for him after a few months, we revisit.

**"What if I want equity later?"**
Open conversation. Not committing to anything now, but a future grant tied to performance is possible.

**"What if you want to bring in another tech?"**
Treadwell hires a W-2 tech, Jesse stays the senior/lead with priority on better jobs. New hire's pay structure determined separately.

**"What about my truck?"**
Treadwell adds hired/non-owned auto endorsement to its GL policy, OR adds Treadwell as additional insured on Jesse's existing commercial auto. Either way, no out-of-pocket from him.
