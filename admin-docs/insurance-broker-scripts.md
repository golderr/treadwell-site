# Insurance Broker Conversation Scripts

Get 3 quotes before binding. Pricing varies meaningfully.

## Opening framing (use with every broker)

"Hi, I'm starting a water damage restoration company in the Inland Empire -- Treadwell Restoration LLC. We're a B-licensed general building contractor doing both mitigation and reconstruction. I'm looking for a coverage package that includes:

- General Liability $1M / $2M aggregate
- Contractors Pollution Liability -- this is critical, covers mold, fungi, sewage
- Hired and Non-Owned Auto endorsement
- Inland Marine for equipment
- Workers' Comp (or referral if not your line)

Can you write this in California?"

## Critical questions to ask each broker

1. **"Do you write Contractors Pollution Liability for water damage restoration in California?"** If no, move on. Standard GL excludes mold and sewage -- pollution coverage is non-negotiable.

2. **"What's the carrier? Is it admitted in California?"** Admitted carriers are regulated by CA Department of Insurance. Non-admitted (E&S) is fine but understand what you're getting.

3. **"What's the per-occurrence and aggregate limit on pollution?"** You want at least $1M/$2M. Some policies offer pollution as a low sublimit ($100K) which is inadequate.

4. **"Does the policy have a fungi/microbial exclusion?"** Some pollution policies still exclude mold specifically. Ask directly.

5. **"What's the deductible structure?"** Per-claim or aggregate? $1K-$5K is normal. $10K+ is high for a new business.

6. **"What's the audit basis -- payroll, revenue, or both?"** Restoration is usually classified at high audit rates. Understand how growth affects premium.

7. **"Can I get a sample certificate of insurance (COI) before binding?"** Adjusters and TPAs need very specific COI language. Confirm the broker can produce it.

## Brokers to call

### National / online options
- **Next Insurance** -- fast online quotes, may not write pollution. Try first as baseline.
- **Coterie Insurance** -- restoration-friendly online, ask about pollution endorsement.
- **Hiscox** -- strong on small business, may have restrictions on water damage classification.

### Specialty / restoration-focused
- **Restoration Industry Association preferred brokers** (search "RIA preferred broker California")
- **Britton Gallagher** -- known for restoration accounts
- **CRC Group** -- wholesaler that places hard-to-write coverage

### Local IE commercial brokers
- Search "commercial insurance broker Fontana restoration contractor" and call 2-3 local independent agencies
- Local brokers often have better access to E&S markets and understand the IE construction landscape

## Red flags

- Broker won't quote pollution -- not the right broker
- Quote is dramatically cheaper than competitors -- check coverage carefully, likely missing key endorsements
- Broker recommends going without WC ("you can be exempt") -- wrong, you need WC for the CSLB license and once Jesse is W-2
- Pressure to bind before you've shopped -- walk away

## When to bind

Don't bind until CSLB license approval is within 2-3 weeks. Paying for coverage you can't use yet is wasted money. The exception: workers' comp may need to be in place for the license application itself.

## After binding

- Get the COI emailed to you with proper language
- Add it to a shared Google Drive folder
- Send to any vendor or referral partner who needs it
- Set a calendar reminder for 60 days before annual renewal to re-shop
