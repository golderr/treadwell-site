# Treadwell Restoration LLC -- Operating Agreement Key Provisions

This is a notes document, not a substitute for the actual operating agreement. Use these to inform the DIY draft, then have a CSLB attorney review for $300-500.

## Entity basics

- **Name:** Treadwell Restoration LLC
- **State:** California
- **Type:** Manager-managed LLC (NOT member-managed)
- **Tax election:** Default LLC taxation initially. Consider S-corp election once net income justifies it (~$50K+).

## Membership

- **Sole Member:** Nate Goldstein, 100% membership interest
- **No additional members** without written amendment

## Management

- **Manager:** Jesse [Last Name]
- **Manager is NOT a member.** Critical distinction. Jesse has management authority but no ownership stake.
- **Manager authority:** Direct supervision and control of construction operations, hiring/firing of field staff, day-to-day operational decisions, vendor relationships, customer communications.
- **Member-reserved decisions** (require Member's written consent):
  - Adding new members or issuing equity
  - Selling, merging, or dissolving the LLC
  - Taking on debt above $10,000
  - Changing the bank, insurance carrier, or registered agent
  - Distributing profits
  - Amending the operating agreement
  - Changing the qualifier on the contractor's license

## CSLB qualifier provisions

- Manager Jesse [Last Name] is designated as the **Responsible Managing Manager (RMM)** for the CSLB contractor's license.
- Manager agrees to maintain the qualifications required by California Business and Professions Code SS 7068.1.
- Manager agrees to provide direct supervision and control of construction operations.
- If Manager ceases to act as RMM, the LLC has 90 days to designate a replacement or the license suspends.

## Compensation

- Manager is compensated as an **employee** under a separate Employment Agreement, NOT as a member draw or distribution.
- Manager has no claim on company profits, distributions, or capital account.
- Member can take distributions of net profits at Member's discretion.

## Capital contributions

- Initial capital: Member's startup contribution (track exact amount).
- Manager contributes no capital.
- Future capital calls: Member only.

## Indemnification

- LLC indemnifies Member and Manager for actions taken in good faith on LLC's behalf.
- Standard exclusions: gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, breach of fiduciary duty.

## Restrictions on Manager

- **Non-solicitation:** Manager agrees not to solicit Treadwell customers or referral sources for 12 months after termination of management role.
- **Non-disparagement:** mutual.
- **No competing CSLB qualifier role:** Manager may not serve as qualifier for any other contractor's license without Member's written consent.

## Termination

- Member may terminate Manager's management role at any time with cause.
- Manager may resign with 60 days written notice (allows time to designate replacement RMM).
- Termination of management role does NOT automatically terminate Manager's employment -- that's governed by the Employment Agreement.

## Dispute resolution

- Mediation first (mutually agreed mediator).
- Then binding arbitration (JAMS or AAA), Riverside County, California.
- Loser pays attorney fees.

## Miscellaneous

- **Governing law:** California
- **Severability:** standard
- **Amendment:** requires Member's written consent
- **Entire agreement:** this OA supersedes prior understandings between Member and Manager regarding LLC operations

## What to ask the attorney to review

When you send this to a CSLB attorney for the $300-500 spot-check, specifically ask:

1. Does the Manager-without-membership structure cleanly designate Jesse as RMM under CSLB rules?
2. Does the indemnification language adequately protect Member from Manager's licensing-related liabilities?
3. Are the Manager restrictions enforceable under California law (especially non-solicitation)?
4. Is the qualifier-related language sufficient to satisfy a CSLB audit?
5. Does the termination structure avoid creating a wrongful termination claim?
6. Should there be any specific language around the Bond of Qualifying Individual?
