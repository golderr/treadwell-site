# Plumber Outreach -- Field Script

Plumber referrals are the single highest-leverage marketing activity for a new restoration company. This is Jesse's job mostly -- plumber-to-plumber rapport works.

## The 60-second pitch

"Hey, I'm Jesse with Treadwell Restoration. We're a new water damage restoration company based out of Fontana -- 25 years of GC background, so we handle both the dry-out AND the rebuild after a leak. Most restoration companies only do one or the other.

I'm here because I know plumbers fix the leak but don't usually handle the cleanup and rebuild. We'd love to be the first call when one of your customers has water damage that needs more than just the plumbing fix.

We pay a referral fee on jobs that convert -- happy to talk through the structure. Can I leave a card and a few extras for your guys?"

## The referral fee structure

Three options to offer (let the plumber pick):

**Option A -- Flat fee per converted job.** $150 per job that becomes a paid Treadwell engagement. Simple, easy to track.

**Option B -- Percentage of revenue.** 10% of the gross revenue on the first 3 jobs from that plumber, then 5% ongoing. Higher upside on big jobs.

**Option C -- Reciprocal.** No fee, but Treadwell sends water damage cleanup work that needs plumbing repair back to them. Pure trade.

Most plumbers prefer A or C. Some bigger shops want B.

## What to leave

- 2-3 business cards
- A one-page flyer (build this -- services, service area, phone, "we work with all insurance")
- A magnetic business card if you have them (sticks on the side of their truck)

## Who to visit (target list)

Aim for 10+ visits in the first push. Prioritize:

### Fontana
- All plumbing companies within Fontana city limits
- Use Google Maps "plumber near Fontana" as your starting list

### Adjacent IE cities
- Rancho Cucamonga
- Ontario
- Riverside (especially older neighborhoods)
- Rialto
- Bloomington
- Jurupa Valley

### Specific shops to prioritize
- Family-owned shops (1-3 trucks) -- easier to build relationships, decision-maker is on-site
- 24/7 emergency plumbing services -- they're getting the burst pipe calls at 2am, they're who you want
- Shops near apartment complexes / commercial buildings -- bigger jobs

### Skip these
- National chains (Roto-Rooter, Mr. Rooter) -- they have corporate referral programs, hard to break in
- Plumbers who advertise their own restoration services -- they're competition
- Anyone who seems uninterested -- don't push

## Follow-up cadence

- Day of visit: send a thank-you text from Jesse's phone
- Week 1: follow up with the flyer or any additional info promised
- Month 1: drop in again with coffee, see if any jobs have come up
- Ongoing: every 4-6 weeks, brief check-in

## Tracking

Add to admin notes (or a spreadsheet) for each plumber visited:
- Shop name, contact person, phone, address
- Date of visit
- Their preferred referral fee structure
- Notes on the conversation
- Any referrals received and outcome

## What converts a plumber from "talked to" to "actually refers"

- A first job that goes well (you don't screw up their customer)
- A referral check (or trade) actually arriving
- A few months of consistent communication
- Word of mouth from another plumber they trust
